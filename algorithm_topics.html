The Master degree Approach

The University of Illinois has an interesting Master Program in Applied Mathmatics has an "Optimization and Algorithms" option

For thier program, they would want to see the mathematics undergrad to have taken:

  • one course in each of linear algebra
  • one course in each of real analysis
  • one course in probability and/or statistics
  • one course in theoretical computer science (non-programming)

For Their Master's Degree, they require four of the following:

  • Graph Theory
    • Text: Introduction to Graph Theory, D. B. West (Prentice Hall), Second Edition
    • Topics:"Properties and Applications of Graphs."
      • Paths
      • Eulerian circuits
      • trees
      • distance
      • matchings
      • connectivity
      • network flows
      • colorings
      • planarity
      • spanning cycles
  • Intro to Combinatorics (This is the Graduate level class)

    • The course description had an amazing list of "books on reserve:"

    • Alon/Spencer, The Probabilistic Method (2e)
    • Anderson, Combinatorics of Finite Sets
    • Brualdi, Introductory Combinatorics
    • Cameron, Combinatorics: Topics, Techniques, Algorithms
    • Chartrand/Lesniak, Graphs and Digraphs
    • Graham/Knuth/Patashnik, Concrete Mathematics
    • Graham/Rothschild/Spencer, Ramsey Theory
    • Stanley, Enumerative Combinatorics
    • Tucker, Applied Combinatorics
    • Trotter, Combinatorics and Partially Ordered Sets
    • vanLint/Wilson, A Course in Combinatorics
    • Welsh, Matroid Theory
    • West, Introduction to Graph Theory

    The course noted the following topics:" a good one-semester background in fundamental and applicable discrete mathematics."

  • elementary enumeration
  • graph theory
  • Ramsey theory
  • partially ordered sets
  • the probabilistic method
  • and combinatorial designs.
  • Numerical Analysis
  • Algorithms
  • Methods of Applied Statistics
  • Linear Programming
  • Nonlinear Programming

    • Additional Courses from the following list are also required:

    • Optimization
    • Control Theory and Coding Theory
    • Combinatorics and Graph Theory
    • Algorithms and Theory of Computation
    • Statistics
